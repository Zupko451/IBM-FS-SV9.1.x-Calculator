IOPS → Throughput: (IOPS ÷ 1 000) × block KB = MB/s  ·  e.g. 150 000 IOPS × 8 KB = 1 200 MB/s = 9.6 Gbps
PBHA (sync): RTT ≤ 1 ms → synchronous, RPO = 0 (metro distance — app waits for the far site on every write). Buffer = write MB/s × RTT ms × 2 ÷ 1 000. Replication follows writes, not capacity.
PBR (async): 1 ms < RTT ≤ 80 ms → asynchronous. Journaling (link keeps up, RPO = seconds) vs Cycling (link lags, snapshot-based, larger RPO). Beyond 80 ms RTT, PBR is not supported.
Link usable ≈ 80%: 10 GbE → ~1 000 MB/s usable  ·  25 GbE → ~2 500 MB/s  ·  100 GbE → ~10 000 MB/s
